I've worked with people making $200K a year
with negative net worth.
Debt. Zero savings. Constant stress.
And people making $30K building real wealth.

[FRAMEWORK — 2:30]
I call it the Wealth Gap.
Wealth Gap = Salary minus Lifestyle.
If you make $100K and spend $100K,
your Wealth Gap is zero.
You build zero wealth.
If you make $100K and spend $70K,
your Wealth Gap is $30K per year.
Over 30 years, that's $900K in potential wealth.
Most high earners have a negative Wealth Gap.
They spend more than they earn.
And finance it with debt.

[PSYCHOLOGY — 3:30]
Here's the psychology that destroys high earners.
When you get a raise,
your brain doesn't think "save more."
Your brain thinks "upgrade."
You deserve it, right?
New apartment. New car. Nice dinners.
So the raise disappears.
This is called lifestyle inflation.
And it's lethal.
A $10,000 raise disappears into a car payment.
A $20,000 bonus becomes a nicer apartment.
Raise after raise. Bonus after bonus.
Your salary goes up. Your net worth stays flat.

[REAL EXAMPLE — 4:30]
Let me show you real numbers.
Person A: Makes $80K, spends $75K,
saves $5K per year.
Over 30 years at 7% growth = $475K.

Person B: Makes $120K, spends $120K,
saves $0 per year.
Over 30 years = $0.

Person A is wealthier.
Same career start. Different systems.
Higher salary doesn't guarantee wealth.
It guarantees higher costs.

[THE FIX — 5:30]
Here's how to fix this.
Step one: Pay yourself first.
On payday, before any bills,
move money to savings/investment.
10% minimum. 20% is better.
Step two: Build the wealth habit.
Make that percentage automatic.
Step three: Ignore the raise.
When you get a raise, don't spend it.
Keep your lifestyle the same.
Add the raise to investments.
