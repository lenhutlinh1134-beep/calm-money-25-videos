## SCRIPT (11 sections, 1,500 words)

[HOOK] I'm about to explain the single best investment account available to most Americans. It's called a Roth IRA. And it's one of the most powerful wealth-building tools ever created. Most people don't have one. Most people don't even know what it does. Let me explain why you need one.

[SELF-INTRO] My name is TONY and this is Calm Money. Subscribe if you want to understand tax-free wealth building.

[REFRAME] First, understand this: a Roth IRA is not an investment. It's a container. Like a box. What matters is what you put in the box. The box itself is special though. Everything inside grows tax-free. Forever. You'll see why that's powerful.

[WHAT IS A ROTH IRA?] A Roth IRA is a retirement account. US-specific. Here's how it works: you contribute money. You pay taxes on that money today. It goes into the account. You invest it in stocks, bonds, index funds, whatever. It grows. 10 years later, you've earned gains. You never pay taxes on those gains. You withdraw it at retirement. Tax-free. The entire amount. No capital gains tax. No income tax. Nothing.

[THE POWER: TAX-FREE GROWTH] Here's the power. You invest $7,000 today. It grows at 7% annually. 40 years later, it's worth $213,000. You take it out. You owe zero taxes. Zero. Normally, that $213K would generate $30K-50K in capital gains taxes. You keep all $213K. This is why it's so powerful. The government is essentially giving you a tax discount on your long-term growth.

[THE NUMBERS: THE MATH] 2024 limit is $7,000 per year. ($583/month). You do this from age 25 to age 65. That's 40 years. Total contributed: $280,000. Total growth: $1,020,000. Total value: $1,300,000. All tax-free at withdrawal. Compare that to a regular brokerage account where you'd owe 20% taxes on gains = $204K in taxes. Roth you keep everything. Difference: $204K of wealth preserved. Just from the account structure.

[WHO SHOULD HAVE ONE?] Anyone with earned income. Any age. Self-employed? Yes. Employee? Yes. Teenager with a summer job? Yes. The only restriction is income caps. 2024: Single income above $161K, married above $240K, you can't contribute the full amount. But most people aren't there. If you earn $60K-$160K, you can do full Roth IRA contributions.

[THE STRATEGY: FILL IT FIRST] Here's how to prioritize. You have money to invest. First stop: Roth IRA. Max it out ($7K/year). This is your first $7K/year priority. Why? Tax-free growth forever. Second stop: employer 401K match. If your employer matches 401K (like 3% company match), do that first, then Roth. Why? Free money. Third stop: max out 401K ($23,500/year). Fourth stop: regular taxable brokerage. Most people do this wrong. They fill 401K first. Roth is less known, so they skip it. Wrong. Roth should be priority one for tax efficiency.

[THE ACCOUNT PROVIDERS] Who provides Roth IRAs? Fidelity, Vanguard, Schwab, E*TRADE, many others. All are fine. Fidelity and Vanguard are most popular. No fees. Takes 30 minutes to open online. Then fund it. Invest in the portfolio we discussed earlier (VTI, VXUS, BND). Set it and forget it.

[THE SPECIAL RULE: EARLY ACCESS] Here's a unique feature: you can withdraw contributions (not gains) penalty-free before retirement. You contributed $7K. It grew to $10K. You need cash. You can take out the $7K. Not the $3K gain. But the contribution, yes. This gives it liquidity that's sometimes useful. It's not an emergency fund. But if absolutely necessary, the option exists.

[THE COMPARISON: ROTH VS TRADITIONAL] There's also a Traditional IRA. You contribute pre-tax (tax deduction today), it grows, you pay taxes when you withdraw. If you think you'll be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, Traditional makes sense. But most people? Roth is better. You're young, lower tax bracket now. Pay taxes now, grow tax-free forever. At retirement, all withdrawals are tax-free. You're golden. Unless you think your retirement tax bracket will be lower (which is rare if you save aggressively).

[ACTION: THIS WEEK] Open a Roth IRA. Fidelity or Vanguard. 30 minutes. Fund it with $7,000. Invest in the boring portfolio (VTI, VXUS, BND 60/20/20). Set a calendar reminder to fund it again next January 1st with another $7,000. That's it. That one decision, repeated 40 years, creates $1.3M in tax-free wealth. Most people skip this. Most people don't know about it. You now do. Don't be like most people.

[CLOSING] So here's the simple truth. A Roth IRA is a tax loophole created by the government to help you build wealth. Legally. The account grows tax-free forever. Withdrawals are tax-free. It's one of the best wealth-building tools available. Max it out. Repeat every year for 40 years. You'll be wealthy. Your challenge: open a Roth IRA this week. Comment: will you do it? And subscribe for more clarity on tax-efficient investing. See you next video.
