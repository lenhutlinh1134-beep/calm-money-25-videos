# VIDEO 19 — 6 Things Broke People Waste Money On

**Tiêu đề:** 6 Things Broke People Waste Money On (They Don't Realize the Cost)

**Caption:** Six spending holes that empty your bank account. Visible. And the invisible 30-year cost nobody talks about.

---

## SCRIPT (11 sections, 1,500 words)

[HOOK] Broke people don't get broke overnight. They get broke through a thousand small decisions. They waste money on six things that seem harmless. But over 30 years, these wastes are worth hundreds of thousands of dollars. Let me show you exactly what they are.

[SELF-INTRO] My name is TONY and this is Calm Money. Subscribe if you want to understand where your money actually goes.

[REFRAME] Here's what most people don't understand. A small waste compounds just like wealth compounds. Spend an extra $5 per day? Seems harmless. But $5/day for 30 years is $54,750 wasted. At 7% growth, it's $230,000 of wealth you didn't build. The math applies to all six wastes I'm about to show you.

[WASTE 1: CONVENIENCE FOODS] Coffee. Breakfast. Lunch. It starts small. You grab coffee for $6. Toast for $4. Lunch salad for $12. That's $22 daily just on convenience meals. Seems normal. Everyone does it. Here's the math. $22/day × 365 days = $8,030/year. Over 30 years? $240,900 spent. But you could have prepared these at home. Cost: $3 daily. That's $1,095/year. Same 30 years? $32,850. The difference? $208,050. That's wealth destroyed by convenience. And if this $208K was invested at 7% instead of spent? It becomes $690,000. One habit. Thirty-year cost: $690K in lost wealth.

[WASTE 2: UNUSED SUBSCRIPTIONS] This is pure waste. You subscribe to Netflix. Hulu. HBO. Disney+. Amazon Prime. Gym membership. Meditation app. Audible. Adobe. You use three. Pay for eight. Americans waste $1,500 annually on unused subscriptions. Average person doesn't even know it. Just auto-renew every month. Over 30 years, that's $45,000 wasted. But here's the real cost. Invest $1,500 annually for 30 years at 7% = $197,000. You lost $197K in wealth. From subscription autopilot.

[WASTE 3: IMPULSE SHOPPING] 80% of purchases are emotional. You're stressed, you shop. You're happy, you celebrate with shopping. You see an ad, you want it. Average American impulse spends $40-50/week. That's $2,080/year just on stuff you didn't need. Over 30 years, $62,400 wasted. At 7% growth? $208,000 in lost wealth. And here's the psychology. Most impulse purchases are still in boxes 6 months later. Unused. You already got the emotional hit. The dopamine wore off. You're left with the bill and clutter.

[WASTE 4: HIGH APR DEBT] Credit card debt. Car loans. Store credit. These are wealth destroyers. You carry $5,000 credit card balance at 25% APR. Annual interest = $1,250. That's waste that doesn't buy anything. It just vanishes. Paying off takes 5+ years if you pay minimum. Total interest paid = $6,000+ on a $5,000 purchase. You basically bought it twice. For a car loan? $30,000 car at 7% APR over 6 years = $6,300 in interest wasted. $40,000 car? $9,000 wasted interest. This is money that could have invested in your future instead of paying for your past.

[WASTE 5: THE LOTTERY MINDSET] Lottery tickets. Scratch-offs. Penny stocks. Crypto gambling. Sports betting. People spend billions on these. Average person loses $200/year on lottery in various forms. Over 30 years = $6,000. At 7% = $20,000 lost wealth. But the real cost is psychological. The lottery mindset means you're not investing. You're hoping. You're waiting for luck. Meanwhile, consistent investors are building wealth. The math is brutal. If you spent $200/year on lottery, that's $6,000 saved. Invested at 7% over 30 years = $200,000. Lottery players have $0. Investors have $200K. The difference? Discipline. Not luck.

[WASTE 6: ZERO FINANCIAL EDUCATION] Most people spend money on everything except education. No courses. No books. No mentorship. Zero financial learning. Meanwhile, millionaires read 2.4 books per year on average. Broke people read 0.2 books per year. That knowledge gap compounds. An uneducated person pays 25% APR debt. An educated person pays 4% mortgage. Difference on $200K debt? $42,000 over 30 years. That's the cost of ignorance. A $50 book that teaches you one strategy could save you thousands. But people spend $50 on coffee without thinking twice.

[THE CUMULATIVE COST] Let's add these up. Convenience foods: $690K lost wealth. Subscriptions: $197K. Impulse shopping: $208K. Interest on debt: $6K-9K (conservatively $7K). Lottery: $20K. Financial ignorance: $42K. Total? Over $1.15 million in lost wealth over 30 years. Not from big mistakes. From small ones. The person who fixes even three of these wastes? They retire wealthy. The person who fixes all six? They're a millionaire.

[ACTION: THE AUDIT] Here's what to do this week. Audit your spending. Go through your last 30 days of bank statements. Look for the six wastes. Highlight them. Calculate their annual cost. Then multiply by 30. Show yourself the real number. The 30-year wealth cost. This visualizes the true impact. Once you see "$690K in lost wealth from coffee," the $6 latte loses its appeal.

[CLOSING] Broke people aren't broke because they're stupid. They're broke because small wastes add up. Every small waste is an opportunity cost. It's wealth you didn't build. The fix? Eliminate three of the six wastes. That alone creates $400K+ in 30-year wealth. Start today. Audit your spending. Comment below: which waste costs you the most? And subscribe to build actual wealth, not just spend less. See you next video.
