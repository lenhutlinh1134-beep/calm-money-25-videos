## PHẦN 2 — VOICEOVER SCRIPT

### HOOK (0:00 - 0:30)

"The SaaS dream is appealing: build something once, sell it infinitely. But here's what most people get wrong. SaaS built without Claude takes 6 to 12 months to build and launch. SaaS built with Claude? 6 to 8 weeks. 

Why? Because Claude handles the parts that take the most time—API integrations, database design, and feature iteration. Today I'm walking you through the realistic path from zero to $500 per month MRR in 90 days, based on actual products built this way in 2025."

### THE IDEA PHASE (1:15 - 4:30)

"You don't need an original idea. You need to solve a specific problem for a specific person.

The best SaaS ideas right now are workflow automation tools. Why? Because they're easy to explain, they save customers real time and money, they have multiple use cases, and you can monetize them immediately.

Here are actual problems developers are solving with Claude Code SaaS right now:

**Marketing content batch processor:** Takes a URL or document, uses Claude to generate 10 social media posts, landing page copy, and email sequences. Price: $29 per month.

**LinkedIn profile optimizer:** Uploads your profile, Claude analyzes and suggests improvements, generates a new headline and summary. Price: $19 per month.

**Code quality auditor:** Paste code, Claude reviews for security issues, performance problems, and style improvements. Price: $39 per month.

**Email template builder:** Describe your campaign, Claude generates 5 versions with A/B testing variants. Price: $49 per month.

Notice the pattern? All of these take 30 minutes of Claude prompting to define, 4 to 6 weeks to build, and can be sold on day one of launch with clear value metrics—save X hours per week, improve Y metric."

### THE BUILD PHASE (4:30 - 7:45)

"Let's talk about the actual build timeline. You're going to use Claude. But not how you think.

**Weeks 1 and 2: Defining Architecture**

You use Claude to map out your tech stack. At this stage, you want boring, proven technology:
- Frontend: Next.js—Claude knows this incredibly well
- Backend: Supabase or Firebase—serverless, minimal ops
- API calls: Claude API directly
- Auth: Supabase Auth—handles Google and GitHub login automatically
- Deployment: Vercel—free tier, auto-deploys on git push

Total infrastructure cost? Zero to $50 per month until you have 100 users.

**Weeks 3 and 4: MVP Build**

Claude can generate your core feature in about 60 to 80 hours of development work. Here's what that looks like:

You provide Claude with your feature specification—what the app does—target user, workflow step-by-step, and UI design inspiration. Claude generates your database schema, API routes, frontend components, error handling, and logging. This is 70% of a launchable product. You spend the remaining 30% testing, refining, and handling edge cases.

**Weeks 5 and 6: Polish and Launch**

Set up payment processing with Stripe—takes 2 hours. Write help docs—Claude assists here too. Create a landing page. Launch.

Total effort: 120 to 150 hours over 6 weeks. That's part-time work."

### MONETIZATION STRATEGY (7:45 - 10:20)

"Most SaaS founders overprice or underprice. Here's what actually works:

You launch with a tiered pricing model:

**Tier 1 (Starter):** $19 per month, 100 monthly uses
**Tier 2 (Professional):** $49 per month, unlimited uses
**Tier 3 (Business):** $149 per month, unlimited plus API access

Most customers land in Tier 2. That's your anchor price.

On day one of launch, you have zero customers and one pricing model. Here's the truth: Your first 20 customers will tell you if your pricing is wrong. Adjust after you hit 20 paying customers.

The launch strategy has five parts:

1. Tell your network: Email plus LinkedIn
2. Post on Indie Hackers: Post-launch community gets 24% trial conversion rate versus 2% from Product Hunt
3. Ask for feedback on Twitter/X
4. Guest post on relevant blogs: If it's an automation tool, post on automation blogs
5. Reach out to 50 people who might benefit: Personalized, not spam

Reality check: 50 outreach emails gets you 5 to 10 conversations, converts to 2 to 3 customers. Do this multiple times."

### FIRST 90 DAYS — REALISTIC NUMBERS (10:20 - 14:15)

"Let me give you realistic numbers based on actual launches:

**Day 0 (Launch):** 0 customers, $0 MRR

**Week 1:** 3 early users, 1 paying customer, $19 MRR

**Week 4:** 12 paying customers, $400 MRR

**Week 8:** 20 paying customers, $650 MRR

**Week 12:** 28 paying customers, $1,100 MRR

This assumes you land 1 customer per week in the first month, 2 customers per week in month 2, and 2 to 3 customers per week in month 3, with an average customer paying $39 per month.

These aren't theoretical numbers. Dozens of solo SaaS founders hit these benchmarks using Claude in 2025.

Once you hit $1K MRR, you have a decision:

Keep it as a side project—$500 plus profit per month.

Grow it to $5K plus MRR—requires 5 to 10 hours per week on marketing.

Sell it—buyers pay 3 to 5 times annual revenue, so $12K to $20K.

Most smart developers do this: They run 2 to 3 SaaS products at $500 to $1K MRR each. Combined, that's $2K to $3K monthly passive income from products that took 6 weeks each to build."

### THE BIGGEST MISTAKE (14:15 - 15:30)

"The biggest mistake people make? They build something for 3 months, launch to crickets, and quit.

The fastest way to go from zero to 30 paying customers is:

1. Build in 6 weeks
2. Launch imperfectly
3. Iterate based on customer feedback
4. Focus marketing for 8 weeks
5. Re-launch with new features customers asked for

Most founders do steps 1 and 2, then stop. The winners do all 5.

Here's your next step: Think of a workflow you do repeatedly that wastes time. Something that takes 30 minutes every week. That's your SaaS idea. Claude can help you build it in 6 weeks.

What problem are you going to solve? Drop it in the comments below."
