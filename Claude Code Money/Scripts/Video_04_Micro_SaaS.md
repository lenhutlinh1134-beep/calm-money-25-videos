One: Each product takes 5-6 weeks to build. Claude handles 60-70% of code. Two: Not all products succeed. Statistically, 3-4 of 5 hit $200 plus per month. Three: After launch, maintenance is light. Maybe 30 minutes per week per product.

**[IDEA SOURCING — 7:15 to 9:00]**

Most people fail at micro-SaaS because they chase the wrong ideas.

Here's the filter that works.

Idea passes if it answers YES to all three:

One: Is it a problem I personally face? You'll stay motivated. Two: Can I build an MVP in 3-4 weeks? Timeline matters. Three: Will people pay $19-$49 per month for a solution? Pricing viability.

Example idea process:

Problem: Every week, I convert CSV files to JSON for API uploads. Takes 2 hours per file. Solution: Build a web app that converts CSV to JSON in seconds. Market test: Ask 10 developers if they'd pay $29 per month for this. Result: 7 out of 10 say yes immediately. Build time: 4 weeks. Launch: Sell to those 7 immediately.

**[LEAN LAUNCH — 9:00 to 11:00]**

Day 1: Ship with 80% feature completeness. Core feature works perfectly. Edge cases handled later. Basic UI, not pretty, but functional.

Day 1-7: Land 5-10 early users. Tell your network. Post on Indie Hackers. Email a few relevant communities.

Day 8-30: Iterate based on feedback. Add features customers ask for. Fix bugs customers report. Refine pricing if needed.

Day 31 and beyond: Steady-state maintenance. 2-3 hours per week. Respond to customer questions. Add 1-2 features per month based on feedback.

**[MONETIZATION — 11:00 to 13:00]**

For micro-SaaS, the pricing model matters less than getting customers.

Recommended model:

Basic tier: $19 per month, limited usage. Professional: $49 per month, unlimited usage. Business: $99 per month, API access plus white label.

Most customers land in Professional. That's your anchor.

To get from 0 to 15-20 customers, equals $285-$980 per month:

Week 1: Tell your network, get 2-3 early customers. Week 2-3: Post on communities, get 5-7 more. Week 4 and beyond: Content marketing and SEO get 1-2 per week.

You literally don't pay anything until you have revenue. Then you only pay percentage of revenue.

**[PSYCHOLOGICAL ADVANTAGE — 14:30 to 15:45]**

Building 5 products teaches you things that building 1 product doesn't.

What sells and what doesn't. How to launch efficiently. How to price correctly. How to handle customer feedback. What you actually enjoy building.

By product 3-4, you know exactly what works. By product 5, you're executing a proven playbook.
