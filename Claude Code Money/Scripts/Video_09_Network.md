## PHẦN 2 — VOICEOVER SCRIPT

**[HOOK — 0:00 to 1:00]**

I know a developer who got 30 customers in his first year. You want to know the crazy part?

He never pitched anyone. No sales calls. No "hey, hire me" emails. No follow-ups. Nothing.

Instead, he spent an hour every day answering questions on forums. Sharing insights on Twitter. Helping people in Slack communities. Writing blog posts about problems he'd solved.

And customers came.

Most developers think sales is uncomfortable because they're thinking about it wrong. They think it's about convincing someone to buy something they don't want.

But that's not what happened here. He became known as someone who helps. When people needed help, they came to him. And because they already knew him and trusted him, they paid.

This is the networking strategy. And it works better than any cold outreach ever could.

**[THE 4 TYPES — 1:00 to 2:00]**

There are four types of relationships that bring you customers.

Type 1: Referral partners. Other service providers who aren't your competitors.

Type 2: Content audience. People reading your blog, watching your videos, following your Twitter.

Type 3: Community insiders. People who see you as helpful in communities they're part of.

Type 4: Online friends. People you've genuinely built relationships with over time.

Most developers focus on one. You need all four. And the good news? You can build all four at the same time.

**[TYPE 1: REFERRAL PARTNERS — 2:00 to 4:00]**

Referral partners are other service providers who aren't your competitors. They're complementary.

Examples for a Claude Code developer:

A designer who needs a developer to code her designs. A marketing consultant who needs automation tools. An HR consultant who needs internal tools. An accountant who needs custom reporting software.

How to find them: Join industry communities. Look for people selling services in adjacent spaces. Comment on their content. Reach out with genuine interest.

The outreach is simple: "Hey [Name], I noticed you help [specific thing]. I build [specific thing]. I often see overlap with what you do. Would love to chat about potential collaboration."

What happens: 10% become direct clients. 60% refer you to clients. 30% become partners where you refer each other.

One partner can send you 2-3 customers per month. If you have 5-10 partners, that's 10-30 customers per month from this one activity alone.

How long does this take? Building the first partner: 2-3 weeks of genuine engagement before reaching out. Building 5-10 partners: 3-6 months of consistent outreach.

This is the highest ROI networking activity. But it requires patience.

**[TYPE 2: CONTENT AUDIENCE — 4:00 to 5:30]**

Writing online builds credibility and reach.

Best formats for developers:

LinkedIn posts: 2-3 per week. Short insights, case studies, lessons learned.

Tech blog posts: 1-2 per month. Deep dives on how you built something.

YouTube: 1 per month starting, then 2-3 per week as you get good.

Twitter or X: Daily posts when consistent.

What content converts to customers?

Case studies: "I built X for Y client. Here's what happened."

Process breakdowns: "Here's exactly how I built X in 3 weeks."

Industry insights: "The state of Claude in 2026. Here's what's changing."

Lessons learned: "5 mistakes I made as a freelancer and what I'd do differently."

This isn't direct selling. It's proving you know your stuff.

Timeline:

Months 1-3: You're building an audience. 20-50 people following your stuff.

Months 4-6: Audience grows. 100-500 people.

Months 7-12: Audience is engaged. People are asking you for work.

By month 8-12, you get 1-2 customers per month from your content audience. People reaching out because they've been following you.

Time investment: 5-10 hours per week of writing.

**[TYPE 3: COMMUNITY INSIDERS — 5:30 to 6:45]**

Be active and helpful in communities where your customers hang out.

For developers:

Dev.to, ProductHunt, GitHub Discussions.

Indie Hackers if building products.

Reddit: r/webdev, r/programming, industry-specific subreddits.

Slack communities: Industry-specific, not random.

Twitter/X: Developer communities.

The play: Answer questions 2-3 times per week. Share useful tools 1-2 times per week. Engage genuinely on others' posts. Never sell directly.

What happens: People notice you're helpful. They follow you. Check your profile. When they need help, they reach out.

Conversion: 5-10% of people you help become customers.

Timeline: Month 6-12, you get 1-3 customers per month from community presence.

Why this works: You're not selling. You're helping. People return to people who help them.

**[TYPE 4: ONLINE FRIENDS — 6:45 to 7:45]**

This is underrated.

Find people in your industry working on interesting things. Genuinely follow their work. Engage with their stuff. Eventually, you become online friends.

How:

Follow 20-30 people in your industry on Twitter, LinkedIn, or their blogs.

Engage with their content: Thoughtful comments, retweets, replies.

After 3-4 months of genuine engagement, send them a message: "Hey, I've been following your work on X. Really enjoyed your recent post on Y. I'm working on similar things. Would be cool to chat."

Chat about your work. See if there's overlap.

Result:

15% become direct clients.

40% refer someone to you.

30% become partners.

Always valuable: Long-term friendship and collaboration.

This is slow relationship building. But it feels authentic and converts.

**[THE COMPOUND EFFECT — 7:45 to 9:00]**

By month 12, if you're doing all four:

Referral partners: 8 people, each sending 2 customers/month = 16 customers

Content audience: 500 people, 1-2% convert = 2 customers

Community presence: 50-100 people know you, 2-3 refer = 3 customers

Online friends: 30 people, 1-2 buy or refer = 2 customers

Total: 23 customers in month 12.

This number compounds. By year two, your network is twice as large. By month 18, you're at 40+ customers per month from pure network.

And you're not exhausted because you're just helping people. You're not in sales mode. You're in "help mode."

**[THE SYSTEMS APPROACH — 9:00 to 10:00]**

Build these four in parallel, not sequentially.

Month 1:

Week 1-2: Reach out to 5 potential referral partners.

Week 2-4: Write 1 blog post, post on LinkedIn 2 times.

Month 2:

Week 1-2: Reach out to 5 more referral partners.

Week 3-4: Write 1 blog post, post on LinkedIn 2 times, answer questions in 1 community 3 times.

Month 3+: Keep all four going.

Referral partner outreach: 2-3 per month.

Content: 1 blog post + LinkedIn 2x per week.

Community: Answer questions 3x per week.

Online friends: Engage with 5-10 people per week.

This is 5-8 hours per week systematically distributed.

And the best part? It all feeds into your business. Every relationship you build is potential revenue.

**[WHY GENUINE HELP MATTERS — 10:00 to 11:00]**

Here's the one rule that makes or breaks networking:

You must be genuinely helpful and not expect immediate return.

If you help someone with the mindset of "I'm helping so they'll buy from me," they feel it. Your network will fail.

The correct mindset: "I enjoy helping people. If it leads to business, great. If not, I still helped someone."

This is harder than it sounds. Because your goal is to make money. But people sense transactional relationships immediately.

The developers who build the strongest networks? They're the ones who help for free for 6-12 months before asking for anything.

That's the secret. There is no trick. Just consistent, genuine helpfulness.

**[MEASURING WHAT'S WORKING — 11:00 to 12:00]**

Track these metrics:

Referral partners: How many do you have? How many referrals per month from each?

Content followers: Growing by 50+ per month? If not, your content isn't resonating.

Community reputation: Being recognized when you show up? Getting DMs from people thanking you for your help?

Online friends: Regular interactions? Genuine relationships?

If any are flat, adjust.

If referral partners aren't referring, adjust your pitch or the relationship.

If content isn't growing, try different formats or topics.

If community isn't noticing you, increase frequency.

**[CLOSING — 12:00]**

Your first week action:

1. Identify 10 potential referral partners in your space.

2. Write 1 LinkedIn post about something you learned.

3. Find 1 community where your customers hang out, answer 1 question.

4. Follow 5 people in your industry on Twitter or LinkedIn.

That's it. Compound over 12 months.
