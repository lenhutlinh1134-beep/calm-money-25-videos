### Video 6: Getting Your First Customer — The Unsexy Truth About Sales

**Title:** "How to Get Your First 10 Customers (It's Not Magic, It's Boring)"

**Hook (0-30 seconds):**
"Most developers think sales is manipulation. It's not. It's telling five people about your product, one of them says yes, boom—customer. That's it. Here's exactly how to do it."

**Main Script (1,500-1,800 words):**

"The biggest fear developers have when launching products isn't technical. It's 'How do I get people to care?'

Here's the truth: Getting your first 10 customers has nothing to do with marketing. It has everything to do with who you know and asking for help.

**THE UNSEXY PATH TO 10 CUSTOMERS**

Step 1: Tell your network (5 people)
'Hey, I built this tool. It does X. Want to try it free for a month?'

Response: 1-2 people try it, 0-1 actually use it.

Step 2: Tell your online network (post on Indie Hackers)
You write: 'Built a tool that summarizes emails using Claude. Looking for feedback. Free for first 50 users.'

Response: 3-5 people try it, 1-2 keep using it, 1 pays.

Step 3: Tell communities where your customers live
If it's a dev tool, post on Dev.to, Reddit /r/webdev, relevant Slack communities
If it's a marketing tool, post on Marketing online communities

Response: 2-5 more customers.

Step 4: Cold email 20-30 relevant people
'I built a tool for [specific problem]. Noticed you [relevant thing]. Thought you might find it useful.'

Response: 1-2 conversations, 0-1 customer.

Step 5: Ask customers for referrals
'Know anyone who'd benefit from this? I'll give you $25 credit for referrals that convert.'

Response: 1-3 referral customers within month.

Total effort: ~8 hours
Total customers by week 4: 5-12 customers
Total revenue: $200-$600/month

Done.

**WHY THIS WORKS**

This approach works because:
1. You're talking to the right people (small, targeted audience)
2. You're not trying to convince skeptics (just ask people who might benefit)
3. You're solving a real problem (not trying to create demand)
4. You're asking for help (people like helping)

**THE NETWORK STEP IN DETAIL**

Most developers skip this. Don't.

Send an email to 5 people you know:
- Former colleagues
- Friends who code
- People in your Slack communities
- College classmates
- People you've helped before

Subject: 'Quick question: would you use this?'

Email:
'Hey [Name],

I just built a quick tool that [does X]. It basically [benefit in one sentence].

I'm looking for people to try it for free and give feedback. You came to mind because [specific reason relevant to them].

Want to give it a shot? Just reply with yes and I'll send you access.

[Your name]'

Of 5 emails:
- 1 person will say yes immediately
- 2 will ignore it
- 1 will say maybe
- 1 will say no thanks

From the 1 yes + 1 maybe, you get 1-2 users. One of them might convert to paying.

**THE INDIE HACKERS POST**

Indie Hackers has 24% trial conversion rate vs 2% from Product Hunt. This matters.

Your post:
- Title: 'Built a [type of tool] with Claude — would love feedback' (specific, not clickbait)
- Description: 2 paragraphs. What it does. What problems it solves. Link to product.
- Screenshot: Show the product working
- Specificity: 'Free for first 50 users / Free month if you give feedback'

You'll get:
- 5-10 clicks from the post
- 2-4 people try it
- 1-2 people use it
- Usually 1 paying customer from this

Time investment: 20 minutes to write, 30 minutes to moderate comments.

**THE COMMUNITY POSTING (DEV TOOLS EXAMPLE)**

If you built a developer tool:

Reddit /r/webdev:
- 'Built a [tool] using Claude that [benefit]. Looking for feedback.'
- Expected: 10-20 upvotes, 2-3 users

Dev.to:
- Write a 5-minute blog post about your process ('I built X with Claude in 3 weeks')
- Mention your tool as the result
- Expected: 50-100 views, 2-5 users

Relevant Slack communities (Laravel, React, Python communities):
- Post: 'Shared my new tool in [channel]. Would love thoughts.'
- Expected: 1-2 conversations, 0-1 customer

Twitter/X if you have audience:
- Thread: 'Built a tool that [does X]. Here's how. Free access in replies.'
- Expected: Varies, but usually 2-5 users if you have 5K+ followers

Estimated reach: 50-100 interested people, 3-8 actual users, 1-2 customers.

**THE COLD EMAIL STEP**

This is scary but essential.

Find 20-30 people who would genuinely benefit:
- Use LinkedIn search: 'Product managers at SaaS companies'
- Look at product review sites: Who's looking for this type of tool?
- Find communities: Who's asking for this solution?

Email template:

Subject: 'Tool to solve [specific problem]'

Email:
'Hi [Name],

I built a tool that [specific benefit]. I saw your [LinkedIn post/recent role] about [relevant thing] and thought it might help.

It basically [how it works in one sentence].

Free access for feedback here: [link]

Either way, would love to hear your thoughts.

[Your name]'

Of 20-30 emails:
- 1-2 people respond
- 1 might try it
- Usually 0-1 convert to customer

Why bother then? Because it establishes relationships. That person might not buy, but they might refer someone who does.

**THE REFERRAL STEP**

This is the compounding piece.

Once you have 3-5 customers, email them:
'Hey [Customer Name],

Thanks for using [tool]. You've been great.

Quick ask: Know anyone else in [industry] who'd benefit?

I'll give you [one month free / $25 credit] for any referral that converts.

[Link to share]'

By month 2-3, referrals become your biggest driver. Why? Because satisfied customers are better salespeople than you are.

**WHY DEVELOPERS FAIL AT THIS**

Pattern I see constantly:
- Developer builds product
- Launches to Product Hunt (2% conversion, usually 0 customers)
- Gets discouraged
- Quits

The successful pattern:
- Developer builds product
- Tells network (gets 1-2 customers)
- Posts on Indie Hackers (gets 1-2 more)
- Posts in communities (gets 2-3 more)
- Gets referrals (compounds from there)

**THE TIMELINE**

Week 1: Launch, tell 5 people → 1-2 customers, $50-100/month
Week 2: Post on Indie Hackers → 3-4 customers, $150-200/month
Week 3-4: Post in communities → 5-7 customers, $250-400/month
Week 5-6: Cold email + referrals → 8-15 customers, $400-700/month

Month 2: Repeat process for expansion, hit 20+ customers, $500-1000/month

This is realistic. Not marketing genius, not viral. Just systematic reach.

**WHAT YOU'RE ACTUALLY DOING**

You're not 'doing sales.' You're:
1. Telling people about your product
2. Asking for feedback
3. Making it easy to try
4. Following up when they show interest
5. Asking satisfied customers for referrals

That's it. That's sales for a small product.

**THE CONVERSION RATE REALITY**

- 100 people see your tool → 10-20 try it (10-20%)
- 20 people try it → 3-5 become regular users (15-25%)
- 5 people are regular users → 2-3 become paying (40-60%)

So 100 interested people → 2-3 paying customers.

This is why you need systematic reach. If you want 10 customers, you need to reach 300-500 interested people. Sounds like a lot, but it's not. It's:
- 5 people in your network
- 20 people from Indie Hackers
- 50 people from Reddit/Dev.to
- 30 people from cold email
- 20 people from referrals

Spread over 4 weeks, that's 30-40 people per week. Totally doable.

**YOUR NEXT STEP**

Open your product launch list. Put these in order:
1. Email 5 people you know
2. Write Indie Hackers post
3. Post on Dev.to and Reddit
4. Cold email 20-30 relevant people
5. Set up referral program

Don't overthink. Just do step 1 today."

**Key Data Points:**
1. Indie Hackers shows 24% trial conversion vs 2% from Product Hunt
2. Freelancers with AI mentions get 38% more project invitations
3. Referral customers have 25% higher lifetime value than direct
