## PHẦN 2 — VOICEOVER SCRIPT

**[HOOK — 0:00 to 1:00]**

The biggest fear developers have when launching products isn't technical. It's, how do I get people to care?

Here's the truth: Getting your first 10 customers has nothing to do with marketing. It has everything to do with who you know and asking for help.

Most developers think sales is manipulation. It's not. It's telling five people about your product, one of them says yes, boom—customer. That's it.

**[UNSEXY PATH — 1:00 to 2:15]**

Most developers try Product Hunt, get zero traction, and quit. They're doing it backwards.

The fastest path to 10 customers:

One: Tell your network, 5 people. Two: Post on Indie Hackers. Three: Post in communities. Four: Cold email 20-30 relevant people. Five: Ask early customers for referrals.

Total: 8-15 customers in 4 weeks without any marketing budget. No viral growth. No tricks. Just systematic reach.

**[NETWORK STEP — 2:15 to 3:30]**

Step 1: Tell your network.

Send an email to 5 people you know:

Former colleagues. Friends who code. People in your Slack communities. College classmates. People you've helped before.

Subject: Quick question: would you use this?

Email:

Hey Name, I just built a quick tool that does X. It basically does benefit in one sentence. I'm looking for people to try it for free and give feedback. You came to mind because specific reason relevant to them. Want to give it a shot? Just reply with yes and I'll send you access.

Your name.

Of 5 emails: 1 person will say yes immediately. 2 will ignore it. 1 will say maybe. 1 will say no thanks.

From the 1 yes plus 1 maybe, you get 1-2 users. One of them might convert to paying.

**[INDIE HACKERS — 3:30 to 5:00]**

Step 2: Post on Indie Hackers.

Indie Hackers has 24% trial conversion rate versus 2% from Product Hunt. This matters.

Your post:

Title: Built a type of tool with Claude. Would love feedback. Specific, not clickbait. Description: 2 paragraphs. What it does. What problems it solves. Link to product. Screenshot: Show the product working. Specificity: Free for first 50 users, or free month if you give feedback.

You'll get:

5-10 clicks from the post. 2-4 people try it. 1-2 people use it. Usually 1 paying customer from this.

Time investment: 20 minutes to write, 30 minutes to moderate comments.

**[COMMUNITY POSTING — 5:00 to 6:30]**

Step 3: Community posting. Developer tools example.

Reddit /r/webdev: Built a tool using Claude that does benefit. Looking for feedback. Expected: 10-20 upvotes, 2-3 users.

Dev.to: Write a 5-minute blog post about your process. I built X with Claude in 3 weeks. Mention your tool as the result. Expected: 50-100 views, 2-5 users.

Relevant Slack communities: Laravel, React, Python communities. Post: Shared my new tool in channel. Would love thoughts. Expected: 1-2 conversations, 0-1 customer.

Twitter/X if you have audience: Thread: Built a tool that does X. Here's how. Free access in replies. Expected: Varies, but usually 2-5 users if you have 5K plus followers.

Estimated reach: 50-100 interested people, 3-8 actual users, 1-2 customers.

**[COLD EMAIL — 6:30 to 8:00]**

Step 4: Cold email.

This is scary but essential.

Find 20-30 people who would genuinely benefit:

Use LinkedIn search: Product managers at SaaS companies. Look at product review sites: Who's looking for this type of tool? Find communities: Who's asking for this solution?

Email template:

Subject: Tool to solve specific problem.

Email:

Hi Name, I built a tool that does specific benefit. I saw your LinkedIn post, recent role about relevant thing and thought it might help. It basically does how it works in one sentence. Free access for feedback here: link. Either way, would love to hear your thoughts. Your name.

Of 20-30 emails: 1-2 people respond. 1 might try it. Usually 0-1 convert to customer.

Why bother then? Because it establishes relationships. That person might not buy, but they might refer someone who does.

**[REFERRAL PROGRAM — 8:00 to 9:15]**

Step 5: Referral program.

Once you have 3-5 customers, email them:

Hey Customer Name, Thanks for using tool. You've been great. Quick ask: Know anyone else in industry who'd benefit? I'll give you one month free, or $25 credit for any referral that converts. Link to share.

By month 2-3, referrals become your biggest driver. Why? Because satisfied customers are better salespeople than you are.

**[CONVERSION RATES — 9:15 to 10:45]**

Let me give you the conversion rate reality.

100 people see your tool. 10-20 try it. That's 10-20%.

20 people try it. 3-5 become regular users. That's 15-25%.

5 people are regular users. 2-3 become paying. That's 40-60%.

So 100 interested people equals 2-3 paying customers.

This is why you need systematic reach. If you want 10 customers, you need to reach 300-500 interested people.

Sounds like a lot, but it's not:

5 people in your network. 20 people from Indie Hackers. 50 people from Reddit and Dev.to. 30 people from cold email. 20 people from referrals.

Spread over 4 weeks, that's 30-40 people per week. Totally doable.

**[TIMELINE — 10:45 to 12:00]**

Week 1: Launch, tell 5 people. 1-2 customers, $50-100 per month.

Week 2: Post on Indie Hackers. 3-4 customers, $150-200 per month.

Week 3-4: Post in communities. 5-7 customers, $250-400 per month.

Week 5-6: Cold email plus referrals. 8-15 customers, $400-700 per month.

Month 2: Repeat process for expansion, hit 20 plus customers, $500-1000 per month.

This is realistic. Not marketing genius, not viral. Just systematic reach.

**[WHY DEVELOPERS FAIL — 12:00 to 13:30]**

Pattern I see constantly:

Developer builds product. Launches to Product Hunt. 2% conversion, usually 0 customers. Gets discouraged. Quits.

The successful pattern:

Developer builds product. Tells network. Gets 1-2 customers. Posts on Indie Hackers. Gets 1-2 more. Posts in communities. Gets 2-3 more. Gets referrals. Compounds from there.
