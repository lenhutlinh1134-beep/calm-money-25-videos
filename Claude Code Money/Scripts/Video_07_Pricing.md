"Pricing is where most indie developers leave 90% of their money on the table.

Either they underprice (no revenue per customer, never hit profitability) or they overprice (no one buys).

Here's the data-driven approach that works.

**THE PRICING PSYCHOLOGY**

Counterintuitive fact: Higher prices often convert better than lower prices.

Why? Because:
1. Price signals quality
2. Lower prices attract price-sensitive customers (bad reviews, high churn)
3. Higher prices attract serious customers (stay longer, less support)

Example from 2025 indie SaaS data:
- Product A at $9/month: 40 signups, 5 conversions (12.5%), 3-month churn
- Product B (identical) at $39/month: 8 signups, 4 conversions (50%), 18-month churn

Product B made more money ($156 vs $135) with fewer customers and less support.

**THE TIERED PRICING MODEL THAT WORKS**

Most successful Claude-powered products use three tiers:

Tier 1 (Starter): $19/month
- 100 API calls/month
- Basic support
- For: Hobbyists, freelancers trying it out
- Conversion: 15-20% of visitors
- LTV (Lifetime Value): ~$190 (stays 10 months average)

Tier 2 (Professional): $49/month
- Unlimited API calls
- Priority support
- Advanced features
- For: Small businesses, working freelancers
- Conversion: 50-60% of paying customers land here
- LTV: ~$735 (stays 15 months average)

Tier 3 (Business): $149/month
- Unlimited API calls
- Dedicated support
- API access
- White label option
- For: Agencies, growing companies
- Conversion: 15-20% of paying customers
- LTV: ~$2,235 (stays 15 months average)

**WHY THIS STRUCTURE WORKS**

The $19 tier is your "free trial alternative." It anchors the price. People feel like they're getting value at any tier.

The $49 tier is your profit center. 60% of customers pay this. It's high enough to be sustainable, low enough to feel reasonable.

The $149 tier is your upside. Not everyone pays it, but the ones who do double your revenue from that customer.

Pricing psychology: Each tier is 2.5-3x the previous. This feels like a "jump" in value, not just incrementally more.

**AVOIDING THE BIGGEST PRICING MISTAKES**

Mistake 1: Too many tiers
- 5+ tiers confuses customers
- Stick to 3

Mistake 2: Feature differentiation is too small
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 need clearly different value
- Tier 1: Limited usage
- Tier 2: Unlimited + advanced features
- Tier 3: White-label + API access

Mistake 3: Pricing is too similar to competitors
- You're not competing on price
- You're competing on: speed of delivery, specific use case, customer service
- Price your value, not the market

Mistake 4: No free tier or trial
- Contradiction: I'm saying $19 acts as a free tier
- Offer 7-14 day free trial for all tiers
- This removes risk for customers
- Higher conversion than "free forever tier"

**HOW TO TEST PRICING**

Step 1: Launch with your best guess
- Don't overthink
- $19 / $49 / $149 is the starting point
- You can change it anytime

Step 2: Track what happens
- First 20 customers: What tier did they choose?
- If 80% choose Tier 1, you're too expensive OR Tier 1 is the right price
- If 80% choose Tier 3, you're too cheap
- If split is 20% / 60% / 20%, you nailed it

Step 3: Adjust after 20 customers
- If most choose the lowest tier, lower all prices by 20%
- If most choose highest tier, raise all prices by 30%
- If split is balanced, keep it

Step 4: Iterate quarterly
- Each quarter, test a 10-15% price increase
- Small increases rarely hurt conversion and increase revenue significantly
- Example: Raise $49 to $54 to $59 to $64 over 12 months

**PRICING EXAMPLES FOR SPECIFIC PRODUCTS**

Email summarizer:
- Starter ($19): 100 emails/month
- Pro ($49): 1,000 emails/month + API
- Business ($149): Unlimited + white label

Code quality auditor:
- Starter ($29): 10 audits/month
- Pro ($79): 100 audits/month + reports
- Business ($199): Unlimited + team access

Content repurposer:
- Starter ($19): 10 pieces/month
- Pro ($59): 100 pieces/month
- Business ($199): Unlimited + brand voice training

Notice: Each tier is 2.5-3x the previous. Usage increases by 10x between tiers.

**ANNUAL PRICING STRATEGY**

Once you have monthly customers, offer annual:
- Monthly: $49/month = $588/year
- Annual: $490/year (17% discount)

This works because:
1. 20-30% of customers take it
2. You get cash upfront (helps cash flow)
3. Churn drops (they already paid for a year)

The trick: Make the discount feel real (17% off) but not so big that monthly customers feel punished.

**WHEN TO RAISE PRICES**

Most mistakes: Raising too early or not raising enough.

Rule: Raise prices when either happens first:
1. 80% of customers choose your highest tier (underpricing)
2. 12 months have passed and you have 30+ customers (sustainable)

How to raise:
- Announce 30 days in advance
- Current customers keep old price (grandfather them)
- New customers at new price
- Communicate the value (new features, better support)

Example raise:
- Old pricing: $19 / $49 / $149
- New pricing: $29 / $69 / $199 (33% increase)
- Announcement: 'Adding [new features], expanding support, raising prices January 15th'

**THE ANNUAL PRICING FORMULA**

Most SaaS uses this:
- Monthly price × 12 × 0.8 = annual price

So: $49/month = $588/year normally, but annual offer is $470 (20% discount)

This is simple and feels fair to both customers.

**PACKAGING AND POSITIONING**

Price alone doesn't matter. Positioning does.

Good positioning:
'For freelancers who want to automate their workflow'
'For agencies that need rapid prototyping'
'For marketing teams that need content at scale'

Bad positioning:
'Email summarizer'
'Content generator'

The positioning explains who should buy and why.

**THE DATA POINT EVERYONE MISSES**

From indie SaaS benchmarks:
- Average customer pays $34-47/month
- Average churn is 5-7%/month
- Average LTV is 12-18 months

This means:
- $39 price point × 15 month average lifespan = $585 LTV per customer
- If you spend $100 acquiring customer, you make $485 profit
- If you get 10 customers from your network (free), you make $4,850 profit

This is how indie products become profitable so fast. Low acquisition costs + reasonable LTV = sustainable business.

**YOUR FIRST PRICING DECISION**

Pick your tier 2 price. That's your anchor.

- Utility tool: $29-49/month
- API product: $49-99/month
- B2B SaaS: $99-199/month
- Enterprise: $499+/month

Most Claude-powered tools should be $39-69/month.

Then: Tier 1 is 30% of Tier 2. Tier 3 is 250-300% of Tier 2.

Done. Don't overthink. Launch and adjust after 20 customers."

**Key Data Points:**
1. Proper pricing increases revenue by 30-50% without losing customers (2025 indie SaaS data)
2. Tiered pricing gets 60%+ of customers in middle tier
3. Annual pricing converts 20-30% of monthly customers and reduces churn by 40%
