## PHẦN 2 — VOICEOVER SCRIPT

**[HOOK — 0:00 to 1:30]**

Pricing is where most indie developers leave 90% of their money on the table.

Either they underprice: no revenue per customer, never hit profitability. Or they overprice: no one buys.

Here's the counterintuitive fact. Higher prices often convert better than lower prices.

Why? Because price signals quality. Lower prices attract price-sensitive customers. They complain a lot. They leave quickly. Higher prices attract serious customers. They stay longer. Less support burden.

Example from 2025 indie SaaS data:

Product A at $9 per month: 40 signups, 5 conversions, 12.5% conversion, 3-month churn.

Product B, identical, at $39 per month: 8 signups, 4 conversions, 50% conversion, 18-month churn.

Product B made more money: $156 versus $135, with fewer customers and less support.

**[3-TIER MODEL — 1:30 to 4:00]**

Most successful Claude-powered products use three tiers.

Tier 1, Starter: $19 per month, 100 API calls per month, basic support. For: Hobbyists, freelancers trying it out. Conversion: 15-20% of visitors. LTV: around $190. Stays 10 months average.

Tier 2, Professional: $49 per month, unlimited API calls, priority support, advanced features. For: Small businesses, working freelancers. Conversion: 50-60% of paying customers land here. LTV: around $735. Stays 15 months average.

Tier 3, Business: $149 per month, unlimited API calls, dedicated support, API access, white label option. For: Agencies, growing companies. Conversion: 15-20% of paying customers. LTV: around $2,235. Stays 15 months average.

Why this structure works:

The $19 tier is your free trial alternative. It anchors the price. People feel like they're getting value at any tier. The $49 tier is your profit center. 60% of customers pay this. It's high enough to be sustainable, low enough to feel reasonable. The $149 tier is your upside. Not everyone pays it, but the ones who do double your revenue from that customer.

Pricing psychology: Each tier is 2.5-3x the previous. This feels like a jump in value, not just incrementally more.

**[AVOIDING MISTAKES — 4:00 to 6:00]**

Mistake 1: Too many tiers. 5 plus tiers confuses customers. Stick to 3.

Mistake 2: Feature differentiation is too small. Tier 1 and Tier 2 need clearly different value. Tier 1: Limited usage. Tier 2: Unlimited plus advanced features. Tier 3: White-label plus API access.

Mistake 3: Pricing is too similar to competitors. You're not competing on price. You're competing on: speed of delivery, specific use case, customer service. Price your value, not the market.

Mistake 4: No free tier or trial. I'm saying $19 acts as a free tier. Offer 7-14 day free trial for all tiers. This removes risk for customers. Higher conversion than free forever tier.

**[HOW TO TEST — 6:00 to 7:30]**

Step 1: Launch with your best guess. Don't overthink. $19, $49, $149 is the starting point. You can change it anytime.

Step 2: Track what happens. First 20 customers: What tier did they choose? If 80% choose Tier 1, you're too expensive OR Tier 1 is the right price. If 80% choose Tier 3, you're too cheap. If split is 20%, 60%, 20%, you nailed it.

Step 3: Adjust after 20 customers. If most choose lowest tier, lower all prices by 20%. If most choose highest tier, raise all prices by 30%. If split is balanced, keep it.

Step 4: Iterate quarterly. Each quarter, test a 10-15% price increase. Small increases rarely hurt conversion and increase revenue significantly. Example: Raise $49 to $54 to $59 to $64 over 12 months.

**[PRICING EXAMPLES — 7:30 to 9:00]**

Email summarizer:

Starter: $19. 100 emails per month. Pro: $49. 1,000 emails per month plus API. Business: $149. Unlimited plus white label.

Code quality auditor:

Starter: $29. 10 audits per month. Pro: $79. 100 audits per month plus reports. Business: $199. Unlimited plus team access.

Content repurposer:

Starter: $19. 10 pieces per month. Pro: $59. 100 pieces per month. Business: $199. Unlimited plus brand voice training.

Notice: Each tier is 2.5-3x the previous. Usage increases by 10x between tiers.

**[ANNUAL PRICING — 9:00 to 10:00]**

Once you have monthly customers, offer annual.

Monthly: $49 per month equals $588 per year. Annual: $490 per year, 17% discount.

This works because:

One: 20-30% of customers take it. Two: You get cash upfront. Helps cash flow. Three: Churn drops. They already paid for a year.

The trick: Make the discount feel real. 17% off. But not so big that monthly customers feel punished.

**[RAISING PRICES — 10:00 to 12:00]**

Most mistakes: Raising too early or not raising enough.

Rule: Raise prices when either happens first:

One: 80% of customers choose your highest tier. Underpricing. Two: 12 months have passed and you have 30 plus customers. Sustainable.

How to raise:

Announce 30 days in advance. Current customers keep old price. Grandfather them. New customers at new price. Communicate the value. New features, better support.

Example raise:

Old pricing: $19, $49, $149. New pricing: $29, $69, $199. 33% increase. Announcement: Adding new features, expanding support, raising prices January 15th.

**[ANNUAL FORMULA — 12:00 to 13:30]**

Most SaaS uses this:

Monthly price times 12 times 0.8 equals annual price.

So: $49 per month equals $588 per year normally, but annual offer is $470. 20% discount.

This is simple and feels fair to both customers.

**[PACKAGING AND POSITIONING — 13:30 to 14:45]**

Price alone doesn't matter. Positioning does.

Good positioning: For freelancers who want to automate their workflow. For agencies that need rapid prototyping. For marketing teams that need content at scale.

Bad positioning: Email summarizer. Content generator.

The positioning explains who should buy and why.

**[THE DATA POINT — 14:45 to 15:45]**

From indie SaaS benchmarks:

Average customer pays $34-47 per month. Average churn is 5-7% per month. Average LTV is 12-18 months.

This means:

$39 price point times 15 month average lifespan equals $585 LTV per customer. If you spend $100 acquiring customer, you make $485 profit. If you get 10 customers from your network. Free. You make $4,850 profit.

This is how indie products become profitable so fast. Low acquisition costs plus reasonable LTV equals sustainable business.

**[FIRST PRICING DECISION — 15:45]**

Pick your tier 2 price. That's your anchor.

Utility tool: $29-49 per month. API product: $49-99 per month. B2B SaaS: $99-199 per month. Enterprise: $499 plus per month.

Most Claude-powered tools should be $39-69 per month.

Then: Tier 1 is 30% of Tier 2. Tier 3 is 250-300% of Tier 2.

Done. Don't overthink. Launch and adjust after 20 customers.
