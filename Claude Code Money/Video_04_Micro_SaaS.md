**SECTION 1: WHAT IS MICRO-SAAS & WHY IT WORKS (0:45–2:15)**

Forget the million-dollar SaaS dream for a second. What if you built five products that each made $300 to $500 per month? That's $1,500 to $2,500 monthly income from multiple revenue streams.

This is the micro-SaaS model, and it's the most underrated way to make money as a developer right now.

Here's why it works:

One: Lower risk. If one product flops, four others still make money.

Two: Less pressure. You're not betting everything on one idea.

Three: Faster learning. You iterate through 5 ideas in the time it takes to perfect 1.

Four: Multiple revenue streams. Recession-proof.

Five: Portfolio building. Each product is a case study for freelance or agency work.

A micro-SaaS is a small product with one clear job. Let me give you examples:

Email template builder. $19 per month, 50 customers equals $950 per month.

API monitoring dashboard. $29 per month, 30 customers equals $870 per month.

Content repurposing tool. $39 per month, 20 customers equals $780 per month.

Database schema generator. $29 per month, 40 customers equals $1,160 per month.

CSV to API converter. $49 per month, 15 customers equals $735 per month.

None of these need hundreds of thousands of users. They just need 15 to 50 dedicated customers. That's the whole point.

Here's how a developer executes this in one year:

Product 1—Weeks 1 to 6—Reaches $300 per month by week 16.

Product 2—Weeks 7 to 12—Reaches $250 per month by week 22.

Product 3—Weeks 13 to 18—Reaches $200 per month by week 28.

Product 4—Weeks 19 to 24—Reaches $300 per month by week 34.

Product 5—Weeks 25 to 30—Reaches $250 per month by week 40.

By month 10, you have $1,300 per month from all five products combined. By month 12, you're at $1,500 to $2,000 per month.

That's $18,000 to $24,000 annual income that requires maybe 3 to 5 hours per week of maintenance.

This is realistic because: Each product takes 5 to 6 weeks to build with Claude handling 60-70% of code. Not all products succeed—statistically, 3-4 of 5 hit $200-plus per month. After launch, maintenance is light—maybe 30 minutes per week per product.

Now, without Claude, building 5 SaaS products in a year is almost impossible. You'd need 5-6 months per product minimum. You'd need deep expertise in multiple areas. Constant debugging and refinement would kill you.

With Claude: The timeline compresses from 6 months to 5-6 weeks. That's the entire advantage. Claude generates 60-70% of the code. Handles API integrations. Assists with testing. Debugging is faster. Architectural decisions are faster.

**SECTION 3: IDEA SOURCING & VALIDATION (4:00–5:45)**

Most people fail at micro-SaaS because they chase the wrong ideas.

Here's the filter that works: An idea passes if it answers YES to all three questions.

One: Is it a problem I personally face? You'll stay motivated.

Two: Can I build an MVP in 3-4 weeks? Timeline matters.

Three: Will people pay $19 to $49 per month for a solution? Pricing viability.

Let me walk you through an idea process from start to finish:

Problem: Every week, I convert CSV files to JSON for API uploads. Takes 2 hours per file.

Solution: Build a web app that converts CSV to JSON in seconds.

Market test: Ask 10 developers if they'd pay $29 per month for this.

Result: 7 out of 10 say yes immediately.

Build time: 4 weeks.

Launch: Sell to those 7 immediately.

That's how you validate before you code. You don't spend 6 weeks building something nobody wants.

---

**SECTION 4: THE LEAN LAUNCH & CUSTOMER ACQUISITION (5:45–8:00)**

Day 1: Ship with 80% feature completeness. The core feature works perfectly. Edge cases handled later. Basic UI—not pretty, but functional.

Days 1-7: Land 5-10 early users. Tell your network. Post on Indie Hackers. Email a few relevant communities.

Days 8-30: Iterate based on feedback. Add features customers ask for. Fix bugs customers report. Refine pricing if needed.

Day 31 and beyond: Steady-state maintenance. 2-3 hours per week. Respond to customer questions. Add 1-2 features per month based on feedback.

Now, for monetization: For micro-SaaS, the pricing model matters less than getting customers.

Recommended model: Basic tier at $19 per month with limited usage. Professional at $49 per month with unlimited usage. Business at $99 per month with API access plus white label.

Most customers land in Professional. That's your anchor.

To get from 0 to 15-20 customers—which equals $285 to $980 per month:

Week 1: Tell your network, get 2-3 early customers.

Week 2-3: Post on communities, get 5-7 more.

Week 4 and beyond: Content marketing and SEO get 1-2 per week.

And here's the part about reducing costs: Normal SaaS build stack costs $200-500 per month in infrastructure. Micro-SaaS build stack with Claude costs $0 to $50 per month.

Frontend: Next.js—free to deploy on Vercel.

Backend: Supabase—free tier, then $25 per month at scale.

Database: Supabase included.

Auth: Supabase included.

Payment: Stripe—no monthly fee, only transaction fees.

You literally don't pay anything until you have revenue. Then you only pay percentage of revenue.

---

**SECTION 5: STACKING & THE COMPOUND EFFECT (8:00–9:15)**

Here's the psychological advantage of building 5 products: By product 3-4, you know exactly what works. By product 5, you're executing a proven playbook.

Building 5 products teaches you what sells and what doesn't. How to launch efficiently. How to price correctly. How to handle customer feedback. What you actually enjoy building.

And here's the power move: Run this alongside freelancing.

Freelancing income: $5K to $10K per month.

Micro-SaaS income: $1.5K to $2K per month.

Combined: $6.5K to $12K per month.

The micro-SaaS requires maybe 5-10 hours per week across all five products. You're not sacrificing your freelance business. Plus, building products improves your freelance skills. You become a better architect, faster coder, and more confident in technology choices.

---

**SECTION 6: AVOIDING THE FAILURE MODE (9:15–9:45)**

Most people who try this fail because they try to perfect the product before launch. Wrong. They build one product and ignore feedback. Wrong. They overprice and get zero customers. Wrong. They spend weeks on marketing before launch. Wrong. They give up after 2 weeks without traction. Wrong.

The successful pattern is: Launch quickly, get feedback, iterate, scale marketing.

Commit to this: Next Sunday, pick one problem you personally face. By Friday, have an MVP. By the following Friday, have it launched and get your first customer.

If you do this, you'll have a $100-300 per month product by month 2. Do it 5 times, and you're at $1,500-plus per month.
