**SECTION 1: THE MARKET SHARE REALITY (0:30–2:15)**

The AI market has three major players: Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini.

If you're building a business around AI, your platform choice matters. A lot.

Here's the real data on which one makes you the most money.

Enterprise coding AI market—this is the most profitable segment:

Claude: 54%.

OpenAI and ChatGPT: 21%.

Google Gemini: 15%.

Others: 10%.

This is the highest-paying segment. Enterprise customers pay $5K-50K per project.

General enterprise AI spending:

Anthropic—Claude: 40% of enterprise LLM spend.

OpenAI: 27%.

Google: 21%.

Others: 12%.

---

**SECTION 2: DEVELOPER SENTIMENT & PRICING (2:15–4:15)**

Survey of 500 developers in 2025:

73% use AI coding tools daily.

Of those, 45% prefer Claude for production code.

28% prefer ChatGPT for rapid prototyping.

12% use both equally.

15% prefer others.

Key finding: Developers building businesses prefer Claude. Developers experimenting prefer ChatGPT.

Now, let's talk pricing and cost comparison.

Claude API: Input $0.80-3 per million tokens. Output $2.40-15 per million tokens. Enterprise minimum usually none—pay as you go.

ChatGPT API: GPT-4o $2.50-15 per million tokens—more expensive than Claude. More expensive at scale. Enterprise minimum usually required.

Gemini API: Similar pricing to Claude. Cheaper for input, comparable output. Less widely adopted.

Why this matters for your business: If you build a SaaS product on Claude versus ChatGPT:

1,000 customers, 10 million tokens per customer per month.

Claude cost: About $1,200 per customer per month—input plus output averaged.

ChatGPT cost: About $2,500 per customer per month.

Difference: $1,300 per customer per month.

If you charge $99 per month:

Claude margin: You lose $1,200 per customer.

ChatGPT margin: You lose $2,400 per customer.

Wait, both lose money? Let's be realistic:

Most SaaS use 100K to 1 million tokens per customer per month, not 10 million.

Claude cost: $120-1,200 per customer per month.

ChatGPT cost: $250-2,500 per customer per month.

At 100K tokens per customer monthly:

You charge $99 per month.

Claude cost: About $1.20 per customer—1% of revenue.

ChatGPT cost: About $2.50 per customer—2.5% of revenue.

Claude is 2x more cost-efficient. Not game-changing, but meaningful at scale.

---

**SECTION 3: QUALITY, PERCEPTION, & ECOSYSTEM (4:15–6:45)**

Claude advantages: Better at code generation—trained on more open-source code. Better at complex reasoning—multiple papers on this. Better for business logic—less hallucination. Better for context windows—200K tokens is huge for large codebases.

ChatGPT advantages: Better at creative writing. More API integrations—more partners built on OpenAI. Faster inference, slightly, not meaningfully. More ecosystem tools.

For building business applications: Claude wins. For creative or marketing apps: Either works, ChatGPT maybe slightly better.

Customer perception survey of 200 SaaS users in February 2026:

"Using Claude feels more professional": 68%.

"Using ChatGPT feels more familiar": 52%.

"I don't care which AI, care about the result": 78%.

Key insight: Customers don't care which AI you use, as long as results are good. But in B2B, mentioning Claude is a slight advantage—enterprise credibility.

Ecosystem comparison:

Claude ecosystem: Anthropic Web, Anthropic Workbench, Claude Code IDE. Limited third-party integrations.

ChatGPT ecosystem: ChatGPT Plus web. Tons of third-party integrations—Zapier, Make, etc. GPT Store—mostly dead. Massive adoption in non-dev tools.

For building business applications: Claude is fine, you do API calls yourself.

For marketing or consumer tools: ChatGPT ecosystem might have pre-built integrations.

---

**SECTION 4: MONETIZATION & THE HONEST ANSWER (6:45–9:00)**

Monetization reality:

If you build a SaaS on Claude: Best margins in code and automation category ($99-199 per month). Lowest cost per token. Enterprise credibility.

If you build a SaaS on ChatGPT: More consumer-friendly positioning possible. Higher costs per token. More third-party integrations available.

If you build a SaaS on Gemini: Rarely a first choice. Comparable pricing to Claude. Less proven in enterprise.

Now, here's the honest answer: It doesn't matter that much.

What matters: Quality of your implementation. Positioning and marketing. Customer support. Product-market fit.

What doesn't matter much: Which AI you use—if it's Claude or ChatGPT.

If your product is amazing on ChatGPT, switching to Claude won't make it 2x better. If it's mediocre, same problem.

The strategic answer: Pick Claude if:

You're building for enterprise—higher credibility.

You're building code or automation tools—superior quality.

You want the best reliability and reasoning—better for complex problems.

You care about margins—cheaper at scale.

Pick ChatGPT if:

You're building for consumers—more familiar.

You want to leverage existing integrations—ecosystem advantage.

You want rapid prototyping—faster API, more examples.

You want hedging—ChatGPT is proven, less perceived risk.

The multi-AI strategy: Start with Claude. If it works, add ChatGPT as a fallback.

Code example:

Try Claude API.

If error or timeout: Try ChatGPT API.

If both fail: Return cached response.

This gives you best-case Claude quality at Claude prices. Fallback ChatGPT reliability. Hedging—not dependent on one API.

Cost: Minimal. You only pay for Claude unless it fails.

---

**SECTION 5: THE REVENUE IMPACT (9:00–9:45)**

Over 12 months with 100 customers at $99 per month:

Claude SaaS: About $118K revenue, about $117K profit—1% token costs.

ChatGPT SaaS: About $118K revenue, about $115K profit—2.5% token costs.

Difference: $2K. Not meaningful.

BUT: If you position as "powered by Claude, enterprise-grade":

You attract 20% better customers—less price-sensitive.

Revenue increases to about $140K.

Profit: About $138K.

Real advantage: $20K-plus per year from positioning.

So the advantage is positioning, not purely technical.
