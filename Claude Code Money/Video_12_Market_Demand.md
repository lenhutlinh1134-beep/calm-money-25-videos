**SECTION 1: THE SIX MARKETS & THEIR DEMAND (0:30–3:00)**

Market 1: Enterprise Internal Tools. This is the highest-paying category.

The actual problem: Enterprise companies have outdated internal systems. Manual data entry between systems. Reports that take humans 2-3 hours weekly. Customer data cleanup and standardization. Email and Slack automation for internal workflows.

Who's buying: Operations, IT, engineering teams at enterprises.

Typical budget: $20K-100K per project.

Frequency: Multiple times per year.

Market size: 8,000-plus companies with 500-plus employees.

Demand signal: Extremely high. Enterprise has endless internal tool needs.

Saturation: Low. Enterprise moves slowly; tools are outdated.

Real example: A 5,000-person company has 15 internal tools that don't talk to each other. Data flows manually between systems. Integration project: 6-8 months, $100K-plus. With Claude-assisted development: 3 months, $35K.

Market 2: B2B SaaS Automation.

The actual problem: SaaS companies have internal processes still manual. Manual data entry between systems. Report generation taking humans 2-3 hours weekly. Customer data cleanup. Email and Slack automation.

Who's buying: Operations managers, CTOs, product teams.

Typical budget: $5K-20K per project.

Frequency: 1-2 projects per year per company.

Market size: 15,000-plus SaaS companies in the US alone.

Demand signal: Very high. Every SaaS company has this problem.

Real example: An operations manager at a $10M ARR SaaS company manually combines data from 3 tools into a report every Friday. Takes 3 hours. A Claude-powered integration saves 2.5 hours weekly.

Cost to customer: $12,000 one-time.

Time savings: 2.5 hours times 50 weeks times $50 per hour rate equals $6,250 in value per year.

ROI: They make back the investment in 2 months.

Market 3: Business Intelligence and Custom Analytics.

The actual problem: Custom analytics are expensive to build. SQL query auto-generation from natural language. Data visualization automation. Custom dashboard generation. Business metric calculation automation.

Who's buying: Business intelligence teams, analytics managers, CFOs.

Typical budget: $5K-50K per engagement.

Frequency: Project-based or $499-1999 per month recurring.

Market size: 100K-plus companies with data teams.

Demand signal: Extremely high. Everyone needs better analytics.

Saturation: Low to medium. Enterprise tools like Tableau and Looker exist, but custom solutions are underserved.

Market 4: Developer Tools.

The actual problem: Developers want AI-assisted development built in. Code generation for specific frameworks. API documentation generators. Test case auto-generation. Code quality analysis tools.

Who's buying: Development teams, engineering managers.

Typical budget: $99-999 per month team plans.

Market size: 5M-plus developers globally, but most already using Claude Code directly.

Demand signal: Moderate. Most developers just use Claude Code directly.

Saturation: High. Competitive with existing tools.

Realistic positioning: Build for specific framework or language niche, not general developer tools.

Market 5: Freelance Enablement Tools.

The actual problem: Freelancers need tools to work faster and charge more. Time tracking with automatic invoicing. Client communication templates. Project scoping assistants. Portfolio builders.

Who's buying: Freelancers earning $30K-100K annually.

Typical budget: $19-99 per month subscription.

Frequency: Ongoing subscription.

Market size: 10M-plus freelancers globally, 5M in US.

Demand signal: High. Freelancers always want to charge more.

Saturation: Moderate. Many existing tools, but niches exist.

Market 6: Content Creation and Marketing Automation.

The actual problem: Content marketing is resource-intensive. Blog post generation from research. Social media content repurposing. Email campaign personalization. Copy variation generation.

Who's buying: Marketing teams, content creators.

Typical budget: $19-49 per month individual, $199-999 per month agency.

Frequency: Subscription.

Market size: 500K-plus marketing professionals, 100K-plus agencies.

Demand signal: High, but competitive.

Saturation: High. Many existing tools.

The demand ranking, best to least:

1. Enterprise Internal Tools—highest pay, highest demand, lowest saturation.

2. Business Intelligence and Custom Analytics—high pay, high demand, low saturation.

3. B2B SaaS Automation—high pay, very high demand, low saturation.

Now, where's the actual money?

If you're freelancing: Enterprise internal tools and B2B SaaS automation. $10K-50K per project.

If you're building SaaS: B2B SaaS automation tools and business intelligence. $500-5K MRR realistic.

If you're building an agency: Everything above, but focus on enterprise. $3K-10K per project times 2-3 projects per month equals $6K-30K per month.

**SECTION 3: THE POSITIONING MISTAKE & HOW TO PICK YOUR MARKET (5:30–8:00)**

Here's the positioning mistake most developers make: They say, "I use Claude Code."

They should say, "I solve [specific business problem] 3x faster than traditional development."

Examples:

Don't say: "I build web apps with Claude."

Do say: "I help SaaS companies eliminate manual data entry between systems."

Don't say: "I create automation tools."

Do say: "I help marketing agencies publish 10x more content without hiring more people."

Don't say: "I'm a Claude expert."

Do say: "I help CFOs get real-time business analytics without a 6-month development cycle."

The positioning determines the market, which determines the price, which determines the money.

How to pick your market:

Pick a market where:

1. You have domain knowledge. Saves sales time.

2. There's a clear decision maker. Easy to sell to.

3. Budget exists. People pay, not startup-y.

4. Problem is concrete. Easy to demonstrate ROI.

Examples:

You worked in marketing 5 years: Marketing automation tools. Medium money.

You worked at a SaaS company 5 years: B2B SaaS automation. High money.

You worked at a large company 5 years: Enterprise internal tools. Highest money.

**SECTION 4: REALISTIC TIMELINES BY MARKET (8:00–9:30)**

Enterprise internal tools: Time to first client 6-12 months—slow sales cycle. Time to $10K per month 12-18 months. Revenue potential: $50K-plus per month at scale.

B2B SaaS automation: Time to first client 2-4 months—faster sales cycle. Time to $10K per month 6-12 months. Revenue potential: $20K-plus per month at scale.

Freelance tools: Time to first customer 1-2 months—build and launch. Time to $5K per month 6-9 months. Revenue potential: $10K per month at scale—saturated.

Based on the data: If you want fastest path to $5K per month, B2B SaaS automation. If you want highest potential, enterprise internal tools. If you want recurring revenue, freelance tools or analytics SaaS.

Pick one. Go deep on that market.
