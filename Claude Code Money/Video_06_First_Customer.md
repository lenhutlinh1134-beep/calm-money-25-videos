**SECTION 1: THE UNSEXY PATH TO 10 CUSTOMERS (0:30–2:00)**

The biggest fear developers have when launching products isn't technical. It's "How do I get people to care?"

Here's the truth: Getting your first 10 customers has nothing to do with marketing. It has everything to do with who you know and asking for help.

Step 1: Tell your network. 5 people. "Hey, I built this tool. It does X. Want to try it free for a month?"

Response: 1-2 people try it, 0-1 actually use it.

Step 2: Tell your online network via Indie Hackers. You write: "Built a tool that summarizes emails using Claude. Looking for feedback. Free for first 50 users."

Response: 3-5 people try it, 1-2 keep using it, 1 pays.

Step 3: Tell communities where your customers live. If it's a dev tool, post on Dev.to, Reddit r/webdev, relevant Slack communities. If it's a marketing tool, post on marketing communities.

Response: 2-5 more customers.

Step 4: Cold email 20-30 relevant people. "I built a tool for [specific problem]. Noticed you [relevant thing]. Thought you might find it useful."

Response: 1-2 conversations, 0-1 customer.

Step 5: Ask customers for referrals. "Know anyone who'd benefit from this? I'll give you $25 credit for referrals that convert."

Response: 1-3 referral customers within month.

Total effort: About 8 hours. Total customers by week 4: 5-12 customers. Total revenue: $200-600 per month.

Done.

---

**SECTION 2: THE NETWORK STEP IN DETAIL (2:00–3:45)**

Most developers skip the network step. Don't.

Send an email to 5 people you know:
- Former colleagues
- Friends who code
- People in your Slack communities
- College classmates
- People you've helped before

Subject: "Quick question: would you use this?"

Email body: "Hey [Name], I just built a quick tool that [does X]. It basically [benefit in one sentence]. I'm looking for people to try it for free and give feedback. You came to mind because [specific reason relevant to them]. Want to give it a shot? Just reply with yes and I'll send you access. [Your name]"

Of 5 emails: 1 person says yes immediately. 2 ignore it. 1 says maybe. 1 says no thanks.

From the 1 yes plus 1 maybe, you get 1-2 users. One might convert to paying.

This isn't complicated. It's personal. It works.

---

**SECTION 3: THE INDIE HACKERS POST (3:45–5:00)**

Indie Hackers has 24% trial conversion versus 2% from Product Hunt. This matters.

Your post: Title is "Built a [type of tool] with Claude—would love feedback." Specific, not clickbait.

Description: 2 paragraphs. What it does. What problems it solves. Link to product.

Screenshot: Show the product working.

Specificity: "Free for first 50 users" or "Free month if you give feedback."

You'll get: 5-10 clicks from the post. 2-4 people try it. 1-2 people use it regularly. Usually 1 paying customer from this.

Time investment: 20 minutes to write, 30 minutes to moderate comments.

---

**SECTION 4: COMMUNITY POSTING & COLD EMAIL (5:00–7:15)**

If you built a developer tool, post on Reddit r/webdev: "Built a [tool] using Claude that [benefit]. Looking for feedback."

Expected: 10-20 upvotes, 2-3 users.

Post on Dev.to: Write a 5-minute blog post about your process: "I built X with Claude in 3 weeks." Mention your tool as the result.

Expected: 50-100 views, 2-5 users.

Post in relevant Slack communities like Laravel, React, Python communities: "Shared my new tool in [channel]. Would love thoughts."

Expected: 1-2 conversations, 0-1 customer.

Twitter/X if you have audience: Thread: "Built a tool that [does X]. Here's how. Free access in replies."

Expected: Varies with followers, but usually 2-5 users if you have 5K-plus followers.

Estimated reach across communities: 50-100 interested people, 3-8 actual users, 1-2 customers.

Now, the cold email step. This is scary but essential.

Find 20-30 people who would genuinely benefit:
- Use LinkedIn search: "Product managers at SaaS companies"
- Look at product review sites: Who's asking for this type of tool?
- Find communities: Who's asking for this solution?

Email template:

Subject: "Tool to solve [specific problem]"

Body: "Hi [Name], I built a tool that [specific benefit]. I saw your [LinkedIn post/recent role] about [relevant thing] and thought it might help. It basically [how it works in one sentence]. Free access for feedback here: [link]. Either way, would love to hear your thoughts. [Your name]"

Of 20-30 emails: 1-2 people respond. 1 might try it. Usually 0-1 convert to customer.

Why bother? Because it establishes relationships. That person might not buy, but they might refer someone who does.

---

**SECTION 5: THE REFERRAL ENGINE & CONVERSION MATH (7:15–8:45)**

This is the compounding piece. Once you have 3-5 customers, email them:

"Hey [Customer Name], Thanks for using [tool]. You've been great. Quick ask: Know anyone else in [industry] who'd benefit? I'll give you [one month free / $25 credit] for any referral that converts. [Link to share]"

By month 2-3, referrals become your biggest driver. Why? Because satisfied customers are better salespeople than you are.

Here's the conversion rate reality:

100 people see your tool. 10-20 try it. That's 10-20% conversion.

20 people try it. 3-5 become regular users. That's 15-25% conversion.

5 people are regular users. 2-3 become paying. That's 40-60% conversion.

So 100 interested people yields 2-3 paying customers.

This is why you need systematic reach. If you want 10 customers, you need to reach 300-500 interested people. Sounds like a lot, but it's not. It's:

5 people in your network
20 people from Indie Hackers
50 people from Reddit and Dev.to
30 people from cold email
20 people from referrals

Spread over 4 weeks, that's 30-40 people per week. Totally doable.

---

**SECTION 6: THE DEVELOPER FAILURE PATTERN (8:45–9:30)**

Pattern I see constantly:

Developer builds product. Launches to Product Hunt. Gets 2% conversion, usually zero customers. Gets discouraged. Quits.

The successful pattern:

Developer builds product. Tells network—gets 1-2 customers. Posts on Indie Hackers—gets 1-2 more. Posts in communities—gets 2-3 more. Gets referrals—compounds from there.

Month 1: 1-2 customers, $50-100 per month.

Month 2: 3-4 customers, $150-200 per month.

Month 3-4: 5-7 customers, $250-400 per month.

Month 5-6: 8-15 customers, $400-700 per month.

Not magic. Not viral. Just systematic reach.
