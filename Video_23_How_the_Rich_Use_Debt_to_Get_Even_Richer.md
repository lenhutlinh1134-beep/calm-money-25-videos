# VIDEO 23 — How the Rich Use Debt to Get Even Richer

**Tiêu đề:** How the Rich Use Debt to Get Even Richer (And Why the Poor Fear It)

**Caption:** The rich borrow money to build wealth. Poor people fear debt. Here's the difference.

---

## SCRIPT (11 sections, 1,500 words)

[HOOK] There's a paradox about debt. Rich people borrow more. Poor people fear borrowing. This seems backwards. But it's not. The rich know a secret about debt that poor people don't. Let me explain.

[SELF-INTRO] My name is TONY and this is Calm Money. Subscribe if you want to understand the psychology of debt and money.

[REFRAME] First principle: debt is not good or bad. Debt is a tool. A hammer can build a house or destroy one. Depends on how you use it. Same with debt. Good debt builds wealth. Bad debt destroys it. The rich know the difference. The poor don't.

[GOOD DEBT: THE MORTGAGE] Let's start with good debt. A mortgage. You want to buy a house. It costs $300,000. You don't have $300,000 in cash. So you borrow at 4% interest. You pay $300,000 × 4% = $12,000/year in interest. That's $1,000/month. Seems like a lot, right? But here's what happened: you got a $300,000 house. You live in it. The value appreciates. Maybe 3% per year. $300K × 3% = $9,000/year appreciation. Your cost: $12K/year (interest). Your gain: $9K/year (appreciation). Net: -$3K/year. Seems bad. But wait. You're also building equity. Every mortgage payment you make reduces the principal. After 30 years, you own a house worth $600,000+ (appreciation), and you paid $360,000 total interest. Your net position: You invested $360K and own a $600K asset. You profited $240,000. That's good debt.

[GOOD DEBT: THE BUSINESS LOAN] Rich people also borrow for business. You want to start a business. Cost: $100,000. You borrow at 5% interest. Cost: $5,000/year. But the business generates $30,000/year in profit. Your net: $25,000/year in profit. You borrowed $100K, it generates $25K annual profit. That's 25% return. The interest is only 5%. You're profiting 20% per year on borrowed money. That's how the rich get richer. They borrow at 5%, invest at 20%.

[GOOD DEBT: THE INVESTMENT LOAN] Another form of good debt: borrowing to invest. You have $50,000 cash. You could buy stocks. Or you could borrow another $50,000 at 4% interest, buy $100,000 in stocks. Historical stock returns: 7%. So you earn 7% on $100K = $7,000/year. You pay 4% on $50K = $2,000/year. Net profit: $5,000/year. Your cash earned $3,500 (7% on $50K). Your borrowed money earned $5,000 ($7,000 gain - $2,000 interest). Debt amplified your returns. This is leverage. The rich use it constantly.

[BAD DEBT: HIGH APR] Now bad debt. Credit cards. 25% interest. You charge $5,000. Cost: $5,000 × 25% = $1,250/year in interest. You're not buying an appreciating asset. You're not generating profit. You're just paying interest. Every dollar you borrow at 25% is destroying wealth. You'd need to earn 25% returns to break even. Almost impossible. So credit card debt is almost always bad. The rich have credit cards, but they pay them off monthly. Zero interest. They use debt strategically, not emotionally.

[BAD DEBT: CAR LOANS] Car loans at 7%. You borrow $30,000 at 7%. Cost: $2,100/year interest. Over 6 years: $12,600 total interest. You paid $42,600 for a $30,000 car. But here's the kicker: cars depreciate. Your $30,000 car is worth $15,000 after 6 years. You borrowed $30,000, it's now worth $15,000. You lost $15,000. Plus you paid $12,600 in interest. Total loss: $27,600. This is bad debt. You borrowed money, lost value, and paid interest. Triple loss.

[BAD DEBT: CONSUMER DEBT] Consumer debt is generally bad. Furniture, clothes, vacation. You borrow $3,000 for a vacation at 15% interest. Cost: $450/year. You had memories for $3,000 principal + $450/year interest. That's depleting capital to fund lifestyle. The rich don't do this. They fund lifestyle from cash flow, not debt.

[THE MINDSET DIFFERENCE] Here's the key psychological difference. Poor people think: debt = bad. Dangerous. Avoid it. Rich people think: debt = tool. Good if it builds wealth. Bad if it destroys wealth. They evaluate every debt: am I paying less interest than I'm earning returns? If yes, good debt. If no, bad debt. This simple calculation changes everything. A poor person sees a 5% business loan and thinks "5% cost, I can't afford it." A rich person thinks "5% cost, if I can earn 10% return, do it." Same tool. Opposite mindset.

[THE MATH OF WEALTH BUILDING WITH DEBT] Let's show the compound effect. Rich person: borrows $100,000 at 5%, invests at 10% average return, earns $10,000/year, pays $5,000 interest, nets $5,000/year profit on borrowed capital. Over 30 years: $150,000 profit on the original $100K borrowed. Poor person: never borrows. Earns $5,000/year from $100K cash. Over 30 years: $150,000 profit. But the rich person did this with zero cash. They kept their cash and made money on borrowed money. Who's richer? The rich person, because they still have their original $100K cash PLUS the $150K profit. Total: $250K. The poor person has $150K. Debt made the difference.

[ACTION: UNDERSTAND YOUR DEBT] Go through your debts. Mortgage: good (4%). Student loan: depends on ROI (is your degree earning you enough returns?). Car loan: bad (7% interest on depreciating asset). Credit card: bad (20%+ on consumption). Business loan: good if ROI exceeds cost. For each debt, calculate: am I paying less than I'm earning? If yes, good debt. If no, bad debt. Refinance bad debt or pay it off.

[CLOSING] So here's the truth about debt. It's not good or bad. It's neutral. A tool. The rich use it to amplify wealth. The poor fear it. But fear itself creates poverty. Understand debt. Use it strategically. Borrow for assets that appreciate or generate returns. Avoid borrowing for consumption. Your challenge: evaluate one debt you have. Is it good or bad? Comment below. And subscribe for more truth about money that nobody talks about. See you next video.
