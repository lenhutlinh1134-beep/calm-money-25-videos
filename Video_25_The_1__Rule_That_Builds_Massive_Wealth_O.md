# VIDEO 25 — The 1% Rule That Builds Massive Wealth Over Time

**Tiêu đề:** The 1% Rule: Tiny Improvements Build Massive Wealth (The Math is Insane)

**Caption:** Get 1% better every day. In one year, you're 37x better. Applied to money, this is life-changing.

---

## SCRIPT (11 sections, 1,500 words)

[HOOK] There's a mathematical principle that sounds absurd. Get 1% better every day, and in one year, you'll be 37 times better. 1% a day. 37x in a year. It sounds like a lie. But the math is bulletproof. Today, I'm showing you how to apply this to wealth.

[SELF-INTRO] My name is TONY and this is Calm Money. Subscribe if you want to understand the exponential power of tiny improvements.

[REFRAME] First, let's prove the 1% rule. 1.01 (1% improvement) to the power of 365 (days) = 37.78. You become 37.78 times better. It's exponential math, not arithmetic. Small compounding beats big one-time efforts every single time. This applies to money. Let me show you how.

[THE 1% RULE IN PRACTICE] Here's the math. You invest money. Returns are 7%. Your friend invests at 8%. One percent difference. Over 30 years: you have $650,000. Your friend has $1,050,000. One percent difference in return = $400,000 difference in wealth. That's the power of 1%.

Let's apply it elsewhere. You spend 1% less than you could. You earn $4,000/month. You could spend $4,000. Instead, you spend $3,960. That extra $40/month seems tiny. Over 30 years at 7% compound? $58,000 in lost wealth you didn't build. Still seems small. But what if you find ten 1% improvements? 1% lower fees on investment, 1% higher return, 1% less spending, 1% raise next year, and so on. Ten separate 1% improvements compound together. The math: 1.01^10 = 1.105. That's 10.5% better overall. 10% improvement compounds to massive wealth over 30 years.

[THE FEES EXAMPLE] Let's get specific. Investment fees. You invest $500/month. Actively managed fund charges 0.75% annual fee. Index fund charges 0.05% annual fee. 0.70% difference. That's basically 1%. Over 30 years, that 0.70% fee difference compounds to $230,000. You're paying $230,000 for active management that doesn't beat the index anyway. That's the 1% hidden cost.

[THE RETURN EXAMPLE] Second example: investment return. You invest in index funds earning average 7% return. A small tweak: add 20% bonds for downside protection. Your return drops from 7% to 6%. One percent drop. It doesn't feel like much. Your emotion says "I'm being more conservative, I'll have less growth." The math says that 1% protection during crashes gives you better compounding because you don't panic-sell. Over 30 years, 6% conservative beating 7% aggressive that includes crashes (because you panic-sold) = higher final wealth. The 1% decrease in average return becomes 1% increase in actual long-term wealth because you stay invested.

[THE SPENDING EXAMPLE] Third example: reduce spending by 1%. You earn $60,000/year. You currently save $6,000 (10%). You cut expenses by 1% = $600 more per year. New savings: $6,600. Over 30 years at 7%, that extra $600/year = $1,080,000. A 1% cut in spending creates over a million dollars in wealth. It doesn't feel like much when you're cutting (you might eat out one less time per month), but the compound effect is massive.

[THE 10% IMPROVEMENT COMPOUND] Now here's where it gets crazy. What if you find 10 separate 1% improvements? Maybe your savings rate goes 1% higher. Investment return 1% higher (lower fees). Spending 1% lower. You get a 1% raise. You negotiate 1% lower interest on your mortgage. That's 5 improvements that add 1%. You also reduce emotional panic-selling (another 1%), optimize tax strategy (1%), reduce impulse spending (1%), improve financial education (1%), start a small side hustle for 1% extra income. That's 10 separate 1% improvements. Combined? 1.01^10 = 10.5% better overall.

$500/month savings at 7% for 30 years = $650K. Same person with 10% better outcome (higher returns from lower fees, better discipline, side hustle income) = 1.01^10^n (where n is the compounding period). The final wealth difference is massive. Not millions more. Hundreds of thousands more. From ten small 1% improvements.

[THE PSYCHOLOGICAL SHIFT] Here's the key psychological shift. Most people think in terms of big changes. "I need to earn way more. I need to cut expenses drastically. I need to find the perfect investment." These big changes are hard. They fail. Instead, think 1% changes. You don't need to earn way more. Earn 1% more. You don't need to cut drastically. Cut 1%. You don't need the perfect investment. Just 1% better returns. These small changes are easy. And they compound to massive differences.

[WHERE TO FIND YOUR 1% IMPROVEMENTS] Here's your challenge: find your ten 1% improvements. One: lower your investment fees (0.75% → 0.05% = 0.7% improvement). Two: reduce impulse spending by 1%. Three: increase your savings rate by 1%. Four: optimize your mortgage rate by 1%. Five: reduce unnecessary subscriptions (1% of income). Six: improve financial education to avoid 1% of mistakes. Seven: negotiate a 1% raise. Eight: cut taxes through strategy (1% improvement). Nine: reduce investment panic-selling (1% better stay-invested rate). Ten: side income 1% of your main salary.

[ACTION: THE 1% AUDIT] This week, do a 1% audit. Go through your finances. Where are the fees? Can you reduce by 1%? Where is money wasted? Can you cut 1%? Where are inefficiencies? Can you improve 1%? Where can you earn more? 1% raise? Write down ten separate 1% improvements. Don't implement all at once. Pick one. Implement it. Then pick the next. Each one implemented builds on the previous.

[CLOSING] So here's the truth about wealth. It's not from big windfalls. It's not from picking the perfect stock. It's from tiny, consistent 1% improvements across every area of your finances. Fees. Returns. Spending. Saving. Income. Taxes. Each 1% seems small. Combined, they're exponential. Your challenge: find your first 1% improvement. Comment what it is. And subscribe for more strategies on compound wealth building. See you next video.
