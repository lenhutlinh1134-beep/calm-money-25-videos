# VIDEO 21 — The Lazy Way to Get Rich: Boring but It Works

**Tiêu đề:** The Lazy Way to Get Rich (Boring but It Works)

**Caption:** Exciting investing fails. Boring investing wins. The three-fund portfolio that needs zero attention.

---

## SCRIPT (11 sections, 1,500 words)

[HOOK] You want to get rich. But you don't want to spend 40 hours per week analyzing stocks. You don't want to day-trade. You don't want to learn cryptocurrency. You want to work your job and let your money do the work. Here's the lazy way to get rich that actually works.

[SELF-INTRO] My name is TONY and this is Calm Money. Subscribe for wealth building that doesn't require obsession.

[REFRAME] The rich have a secret. Most of them don't beat the market. Most successful investors' goal is to match the market, then automate. They're lazy, but strategically lazy. They know trying to beat the market is a loser's game. 95% fail. So they play a different game. Automate. Diversify. Rebalance once a year. Done.

[THE BORING PORTFOLIO] Here's the Vanguard Three-Fund Portfolio. It's the laziest approach to wealth. Fund 1: Total US Stock Market (VTI). Fund 2: Total International Stock Market (VXUS). Fund 3: Total Bond Market (BND). Allocation: 80% stocks (60% US + 20% International). 20% bonds. That's it. No individual stocks. No market timing. No research. You buy these three. You automate monthly deposits. You rebalance once a year. That's lazy investing.

[WHY BORING WORKS] Why does this work when exciting investing fails? Because boring removes emotion. You're not watching CNBC daily. You're not reacting to news. You're not buying the hot stock the day it crashes. You're buying mechanically, every month, regardless of market conditions. When markets crash, you keep buying. You're buying at discounts. When markets soar, you keep buying at higher prices. But you don't increase your amount. You're consistent. Consistency beats timing every time. Data shows the average investor who buys and holds beats the average trader 95% of the time.

[THE MATH OF BORING] Let's quantify it. You invest $500/month in the boring portfolio. Over 30 years at 7% average return, you have $650,000. You never looked at statements. You never panicked. You never tried to beat the market. You just showed up. Meanwhile, your friend is trading stocks. He tries to beat the market. He's above average, actually. He beats the boring portfolio 50% of the time. But he also underperforms 50% of the time. On average, he earns 5.2% annually. After 30 years, he has $390,000. Same amount invested. Same $500/month. Boring $650K vs exciting $390K. Boring wins by $260,000.

[THE POWER OF AUTOMATION] Here's the key secret: automation. You don't have willpower. Nobody does. But systems don't require willpower. You set up one automatic transfer from your checking account to your brokerage. $500/month. It happens automatically. You never think about it. You never second-guess it. Ten years later, you're shocked you have $75,000. It happened without effort. This is the lazy way. You're not lazy about discipline. You're lazy about decision-making. One decision, made once. Then automated forever.

[REBALANCING: ONCE A YEAR] The only active thing you do once a year: rebalance. This means checking if your allocation drifted. If you started 80/20 stocks/bonds, and stocks went up, you might now have 85/15. So you sell a bit of stocks and buy bonds to get back to 80/20. This takes 15 minutes. Once a year. That's it. This is a powerful move because you're "selling high" (stocks when they've risen) and "buying low" (bonds when they're cheaper). You're automatically doing what successful investors do, but mechanically.

[THE PSYCHOLOGY: WHY PEOPLE FAIL] Why don't more people do this? Because it's boring. They want excitement. They want to tell their friends "I'm investing in cryptocurrency" or "I found a hot IPO." Those phrases get dopamine hits. "I have a three-fund portfolio" gets eye rolls. But in 30 years, guess who has more money? The person who could tolerate boredom. The person who could resist the temptation to chase trends. This is the real secret of wealth: the ability to be bored while everyone else chases excitement.

[WHEN TO REBALANCE & REVIEW] Beyond the yearly rebalancing, when do you review? Quarterly. Take 30 minutes. Check statements. Make sure money's flowing in. That's it. Don't read financial news daily. Don't watch markets. Don't listen to financial commentators. They sell drama. You need consistency. If you watch daily news, your brain will eventually panic and make a bad decision. So don't. Quarterly review, yearly rebalance, monthly auto-invest. Everything else is noise.

[ACTION: START THIS WEEK] Open a brokerage account. Fidelity, Vanguard, or Schwab. Take 30 minutes. Fund it. Buy VTI, VXUS, BND in 60/20/20 ratio. Set up automatic $300 or $500 monthly. Mark your calendar for yearly rebalancing. That's done. You're now on the lazy path to wealth. Your job is to show up. The market does the rest.

[CLOSING] So here's the truth about getting rich lazily. It's not a secret. It's not complicated. It's boring. Automate. Diversify. Rebalance. Hold. Ignore the noise. Your challenge: set up one automatic investment this week. No fancy strategy. Just start. Comment: how much are you automating? Subscribe for wealth building without the drama. See you next video.
